FIGURE DESCRIPTIONS (for presentation use)
================================================================================

FIG 1: k=1 Accuracy Bar Chart (fig1_k1_accuracy.png)
- Grouped bar chart: Dense (blue), Window (orange), Graph (green)
- Three groups: d=20, d=40, d=80
- Shows graph dominance at d=20 (67.5%) and d=40 (50.2%)
- All collapse at d=80
- Error bars from multi-seed runs

FIG 2: Accuracy vs Hops (fig2_accuracy_vs_hops.png)
- Line plot, x-axis: hop count (k=1,2,3), y-axis: validation accuracy
- d=40 held constant
- Graph line drops from ~50% to ~7.5% to ~7.5%
- Dense and Window flat near chance throughout
- Dashed line at 3.3% random baseline
- KEY FIGURE: shows graph advantage is single-hop only

FIG 3: Convergence Curves (fig3_convergence.png)
- Best-so-far validation accuracy over epochs
- k=1, d=80 condition
- All three methods slowly climb from ~3% to ~4-6%
- Dense edges ahead slightly by epoch 50
- Shows difficulty of high-distractor regime

FIG 4: Attention Sparsity Bar Chart (fig4_sparsity.png)
- Three bars: Dense 100%, Window 15.3%, Graph 2.5%
- Percentage labels on top of each bar
- KEY FIGURE: pairs with fig5 to show "less attention = more accuracy"

FIG 5: Sparsity Paradox Scatter (fig5_sparsity_paradox.png)
- x-axis: attention connections used (%)
- y-axis: validation accuracy (k=1, d=20)
- Three large dots: Dense (100%, 12.1%), Window (15.3%, 11.3%), Graph (2.5%, 67.5%)
- Dashed line at 3.3% random baseline
- HEADLINE FIGURE: "Less Attention = More Accuracy"
- Shows inverse relationship between connectivity and performance

FIG 6: Train vs Val Accuracy (fig6_train_val.png)
- Dual line plot (solid=val, dashed=train) for each method
- k=1, d=80 condition
- Shows train and val track closely (no overfitting after augmentation fix)
- Noisy but upward trend for dense
- Window and graph relatively flat
