================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. HEADLINE: k=1, d=20 → Graph-masked 67.5% vs Dense 12.1% vs Window 11.3%
   - Graph mask provides 5.6x improvement over dense attention
   - Uses only 2.5% of attention connections (vs 100% dense, 15.3% window)

2. SCALING WITH DISTRACTORS (k=1):
   - d=20: Graph dominates (67.5%)
   - d=40: Graph still strong (50.2%) but higher variance
   - d=80: All methods collapse to near-chance (~4-5%)
   - Signal-to-noise ratio overwhelms all methods at high distractor counts

3. MULTI-HOP FAILURE (k=2, k=3):
   - All methods near random chance for k>=2
   - Graph mask shows slight edge at k=2,d=40 (7.5% vs 5.7% dense)
   - k=3 graph: 7.5% (barely above 3.3% random)
   - This is the "sparsity paradox": narrow masks can't reach multi-hop targets

4. ATTENTION SPARSITY:
   - Dense: 100% connections
   - Window (w=32): ~15.3% connections
   - Graph-masked: ~2.5% connections (1-hop adjacency only)

5. EFFICIENCY:
   - Graph mask achieves 5.6x better accuracy with 40x fewer attention connections
   - Per-epoch time: Dense ~4s, Window ~4.7s, Graph ~5.7s (graph slightly slower due to sparse mask construction)
